Systematic Research Methodology & Analytical Assumptions

DOCUMENTATION REF: D&A-QSRB-001
1. Model Validation & Overfitting Prevention

To eliminate selection bias and optimize statistical significance, the framework enforces a rigid, chronological sequence split:

  • In-Sample Training (IS): Strategy parameters are swept, evaluated, and optimized exclusively on the first 70% of the dataset.
  • Out-of-Sample Validation (OOS): Selected optimal coordinates are run on the remaining 30% of "unseen" historical data to detect performance decay.
  • Rolling Walk-Forward Loop: Adapts to regimes by training on a sliding 252-day window and applying optimal parameters to the subsequent 63-day segment.
2. Stochastic Tail-Risk & Ruin Calculations

Daily return variance is modeled dynamically without assuming standard parametric normality:

  • Bootstrapped Monte Carlo: Simulates 100+ randomized forward return paths based on resampled historical trade distributions.
  • Probability of Ruin: Evaluates the percentage of randomized paths experiencing a peak-to-trough paper loss exceeding a 25.0% threshold.
  • Semi-Deviation Sortino: Isolates negative return segments to measure risk relative to harmful downside fluctuations.
3. Execution Friction & Market Limitations

To ensure empirical integrity, the backtester incorporates realistic trading costs and systemic limitations:

  • Commission Modeling: Integrates a static commission charge (defaults to 10 bps per trade transaction).
  • Execution Slippage: Deducts a percentage drag (defaults to 0.05%) from entry/exit close prices to model order book spreads.
  • Sampling Resolution: Uses daily OHLCV bars. High-frequency microstructural impacts, market-impact decay, and borrow fees are omitted.
